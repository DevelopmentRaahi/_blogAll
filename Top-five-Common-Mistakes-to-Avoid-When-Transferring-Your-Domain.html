Top 5 Common Mistakes to Avoid When Transferring Your Domain

By Domgys
|
10 Oct, 2024

Transferring a domain can seem like a straightforward process, but if you're not careful, it can lead to unexpected complications. Whether you're moving your domain to a new registrar for better services or lower prices, understanding the nuances of the process can help ensure a smooth transition. Here, we'll explore the top 5 common mistakes to avoid when you transfer your domain, ensuring your website remains accessible and secure throughout the process.

1. Failure to Unlock the Domain

One of the most common errors during a domain transfer is forgetting to unlock the domain at your current registrar. Most domains are locked by default to prevent unauthorized transfers, a security measure known as domain locking.

  • Why it Happens:

    • Domain locking is a crucial feature to prevent unauthorized transfers, so it's typically enabled by default. However, many users forget this step when initiating a transfer.

  • Consequences:

    • If your domain remains locked, the transfer request will be denied, delaying the process and causing frustration.

  • How to Avoid It:

    • Before you initiate the transfer, log in to your domain registrar account and ensure the domain is unlocked. Each registrar has its own process, but you can usually find the unlock option in the domain management settings. Once unlocked, your transfer request can proceed without hiccups.

2. Not Obtaining the Authorization Code

The authorization code, sometimes called an EPP code or transfer key, is essential for verifying your domain transfer. Without this code, you won’t be able to complete the process.

  • Why it Happens:

    • Many users don’t realize they need to request an authorization code from their current registrar. Sometimes, it’s not immediately available, leading to delays.

  • Consequences:

    • Without the correct authorization code, the transfer will not proceed. This can result in your website being down longer than necessary, causing potential losses in traffic or revenue.

  • How to Avoid It:

    • When you’re ready to transfer your domain, contact your current registrar to obtain the authorization code. Many registrars allow you to generate the code within your account dashboard, but if not, customer support should be able to help. Make sure you enter the code correctly when prompted by your new registrar to avoid delays.

3. Not Backing Up Important Data

While the domain transfer itself doesn’t typically involve your website content or email accounts, it’s essential to back up all relevant data before initiating the transfer.

  • Why it Happens:

    • Many users think that transferring a domain is solely about moving the name, forgetting that DNS settings, email, and other services linked to the domain may be affected.

  • Consequences:

    • If your new registrar doesn’t carry over DNS settings or if there’s a misconfiguration during the transfer, your website could go offline, and you may lose access to email accounts associated with your domain.

  • How to Avoid It:

    • Before you transfer your domain, make sure you have a backup of all important website files, email accounts, and DNS settings. You should also check with your new registrar to ensure that your DNS settings will be transferred or that you can reconfigure them quickly after the transfer. This ensures minimal downtime for your website and email services.

4. Ignoring the Domain’s Expiration Date

Timing is crucial when you transfer a domain. Trying to transfer a domain too close to its expiration date can lead to complications, including failed transfers.

  • Why it Happens:

    • Domain owners often wait until the last minute to transfer their domain, not realizing that an expired domain cannot be transferred.

  • Consequences:

    • If your domain expires before the transfer is complete, your website and associated services may go offline. Additionally, expired domains may enter a redemption period, during which you’ll have to pay extra fees to recover the domain.

  • How to Avoid It:

    • Plan your domain transfer well in advance. Ideally, you should begin the process at least 30 days before the domain’s expiration date. Most registrars allow you to transfer domains without any issues as long as they are renewed. If your domain is nearing expiration, renew it first, then initiate the transfer. This will prevent your domain from expiring mid-transfer.

5. Not Checking for Transfer Restrictions

Certain domains may have transfer restrictions based on their registration date or other factors. If your domain is too new, or if it’s been recently transferred, it may not be eligible for transfer just yet.

  • Why it Happens:

    • Many domain owners are unaware that domains less than 60 days old, or domains that have been transferred within the last 60 days, cannot be transferred again according to ICANN (Internet Corporation for Assigned Names and Numbers) policies.

  • Consequences:

    • Initiating a transfer before the 60-day window has passed will result in the request being rejected, wasting time and possibly incurring fees.

  • How to Avoid It:

    • Check when you first registered or last transferred the domain. Most domains are subject to a 60-day lock following a new registration or transfer, per ICANN rules. If your domain is still within this window, wait until the 60 days have passed before attempting to transfer. Additionally, review your registrar’s transfer policies to ensure there aren’t any other unique restrictions.

Final Thoughts on Domain Transfer

A successful transfer domain process requires attention to detail. By avoiding the common mistakes outlined here, you’ll ensure a smoother transition to your new registrar without risking downtime, lost data, or unexpected delays. Remember to unlock your domain, obtain the necessary authorization code, back up important data, monitor your domain’s expiration date, and check for any transfer restrictions. Being proactive can save you time, money, and hassle when it comes to transferring your domain.
Taking these steps not only helps protect your website and business during the domain transfer but also ensures that you maintain control over the essential aspects of your online presence. So, before you transfer your domain, double-check each of these critical points, and you’ll be well on your way to a successful and seamless transfer!
